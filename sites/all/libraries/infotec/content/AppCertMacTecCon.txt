<p><strong>Chapter 1: References</strong> - Find and use service and support resources quickly and efficiently.</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 2: Underlying Technologies</strong> - Reviews some of the primary technologies used in Apple products.</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 3: Hardware Tools</strong> - Learn how to correctly identify the  tools to used on particular parts during a service repair.</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 4: Software Tools &amp; Techniques</strong> - Locate and properly use diagnostics, tools, utilities and techniques when supporting and servicing Apple products.</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 6: Troubleshooting Theory</strong> - Isolate and resolve issues in a  systematic way</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 7: Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) Precautions</strong> - Practice  correct ESD procedures when servicing computers</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 8: Component Isolation</strong> - How to Isolate and resolve hardware issues  in a systematic way by identifying how power flows through the component.</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 9: LCDs</strong> - Familiarize yourself with the  structure and defects of LCD</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 10: Technician Safety</strong> - Practice correct CRT safety and  hazardous material handling procedures when servicing computers</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 11: Hardware Service Issues</strong> - Learn how to identify hardware  service issues that are common to most Apple systems</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 12: Network Troubleshooting</strong> - Isolate and resolve issues with  wireless networking products from Apple</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 13: Maintenance</strong> - Perform an array of maintenance  duties in accordance with Apple guidelines</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 14: Product Labs</strong> - This module is designed to ensure that  students will correctly remove and install service components in the following  Apple systems: iBook, PowerBook, MacBook, MacBookPro</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 15: Product Lab</strong> - This module is designed to ensure that  students will correctly remove and install service components in the following  Apple systems: PowerMac G4, PowerMac G5, IMacs, mac minis, Xserve</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 16: Practice Handout/Exam</strong> - This module is designed to enable students  to study for the Apple Macintosh Certified Technician (ACMT) exam</p>

<p><strong>Chapter 17: Product Lab (complete) / Class Review</strong> - Final questions, answers, review of class materials.</p>
